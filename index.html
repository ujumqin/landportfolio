Sine Gov's Sample Training Curriculum

This is a sample training curriculum for newer engineers (1-2 years) at Atlassian. These four classes are designed to align and reinforce Atlassian's Core Values. Participants will complete the curriculum in cohorts.

A capstone project bookends the program and serves a few different functions. First, it acts as a check that participants have thoroughly learned the Core Values. Second, it forms cohesion between individuals who may not normally work together, eliminating silos. Third, it can help tame the backlog for any of Atlassian products.

Only the first class has a sample e-learning. You can get more details by reaching out to me on LinkedIn. Thanks for reading!

Providing Excellent Feedback - eLEARNING SAMPLE AVAILABLE CLICK HERE!

Open company, no bullshit

Class Summary

    This short e-Learning provides a framework to think through feedback. Whether it's feedback to correct a wrong or to provide praise, using these techniques should help ensure your message lands with the correct impact.

Success Measurements - Managers to Verify

  • ✓ Participant has contributed excellent feedback during next code review session.
  • ✓ Participant has successfully applied feedback to their own work.
  • ✓ Knowledge check quiz scores (target: 85%+)

Safe Places

Play, as a team

Class Summary

This (virtual) instructor-led course explores the concepts of what makes a good team. Through discussion, exercises, and a short lecture participants will be able to walk away with concepts they can use to help build their teams.

Success Measurements - Managers to Verify

  • ✓ Create a personal charter of principles within 14 days of this class.
  • ✓ Be part of a team before the Capstone portion of the curriculum.
  • ✓ Aid a teammate (not just do their work) on a task within 180 days of this class.

Pulling the Thread

Build with heart and balance

Class Summary

This simulation is designed to walk newer engineers through a seemingly simple problem. As they work through the problem they will need to dig deep to successfully diagnose and fix the issue presented. No coding required, but knowledge of coding practices is necessary.

Success Measurements - Managers to Verify

  • ✓ Successfully pass the simulation
  • ✓ Demonstrate "pulling the thread" behavior on at least one JIRA issue within 180 days of this class.

Kaizen

Be the change you seek

Class Summary

This blended class (virtual + e-learning) introduces the concept of Kaizen. The e-learning component introduces the philosophy and the approach with a short component to practice. The virtual class will reinforce the concepts and feature a guest speaker that will provide context for the current challenges Atlassian is facing.

Success Measurements - Managers to Verify

  • ✓ Have a backlog issue selected for the Capstone.
  • ✓ Identify a novel workflow issue within 180 days of this class.
  • ✓ Used the Kaizen model in their workflow.

Capstone Project Final Stage

Demonstrate comprehensive mastery of the Core Values

Capstone Summary

In teams of 2-3, groups will write up a proposal to address an interesting bug or feature in the backlog. The team will write up a quick proposal of the issue, the benefits of solving it, and the potential scope for addressing the issue. Teams will deliver a 10 minute presentation on their proposal to a panel with 10 minutes for questions. Evaluators will measure success based on criteria to the right.

Success Measurements - Panel to Verify

  • ✓ Don't #@!% the customer: How many customers will be happy as a result of this?
  • ✓ Be the change you seek: Does this continuously improve our product?
  • ✓ Play, as a team: Was the proposal cohesive? Did it play to everyone's strengths?
  • ✓ Build with heart and balance: Did the proposal consider all aspects? Are there any potential risks in attempting the idea in the proposal.
